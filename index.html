What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices like smartphones, computers, and tablets. It includes sending, posting, or sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone else.

Why It Matters

Cyberbullying can cause serious emotional and psychological harm. Unlike traditional bullying, it can happen 24/7 and reach victims anywhere they go.

Messages and images can be posted anonymously and distributed quickly to a wide audience, making it difficult to trace the source and incredibly harmful to the victim.

Understanding cyberbullying is the first step toward preventing it and creating a safer online environment for everyone.

The Impact is Real

Cyberbullying affects mental health, academic performance, and overall well-being. Victims may experience anxiety, depression, and decreased self-esteem.

The anonymous nature of online interactions can embolden bullies and make victims feel helpless. But there are ways to fight back and protect yourself.

Prevention & Safety Actions

Take control of your online safety with these practical steps

Protect Your Privacy

Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication on all accounts

ACTION STEPS:

  • Change passwords regularly
  • Don't share personal info publicly
  • Review privacy settings monthly

Think Before You Post

Once something is online, it can be difficult to remove completely

ACTION STEPS:

  • Pause before sharing
  • Consider long-term consequences
  • Ask yourself: would I say this in person?
üö´

Block and Report

Don't engage with bullies - block them and report the behavior

ACTION STEPS:

  • Use platform reporting tools
  • Save evidence (screenshots)
  • Block harassers immediately
üë§

Be Selective with Friends

Only accept friend requests from people you know and trust

ACTION STEPS:

  • Verify accounts before accepting
  • Clean up your friends list
  • Be cautious with strangers
üíú

Seek Support

Talk to someone you trust - you don't have to face this alone

ACTION STEPS:

  • Tell a trusted adult
  • Contact support hotlines
  • Join support communities
üì∑

Control Your Digital Footprint

Be mindful of what photos and information you share online

ACTION STEPS:

  • Review tagged photos
  • Limit location sharing
  • Google yourself regularly

If You're in Immediate Danger

If you're experiencing threats of violence or feel unsafe, contact local authorities immediately. Save all evidence and reach out to trusted adults, school counselors, or crisis hotlines for support.
